III. My favourite part of the collection

When I explored the details of Anglo-Saxon ship burial at Sutton Hoo, I generate some interests about the owner of the burial. So then I watched a video which called Curator's Corner with Sue Brunning. It was so eye-catching. Brunning's study is about that the owner was left-handed, with patterns of wear indicating it was worn on the right side and carried in the left hand.

The most fascinating part of the video is that Brunning extrapolates that being left-handed could have provided an advantage in battle as most combatants might be anticipating a right-handed attack. For me, I had a little electrical moment when I first heard about this theory. That is because, her hypothesis seems simple to understand, but it's very practical and quite hard to propose. It would be hard to think from this perspective if you don't have any boxing experience, or more importantly, if you couldn't use your imagination fantastically.

Her theory made me believe that these objects in the burial might look like sitting quietly, but they're not actually quiet objects. They're loud with information about the people in the past.
